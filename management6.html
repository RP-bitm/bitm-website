📘 UNIT 6: ETHICS, SOCIAL RESPONSIBILITY & CONTEMPORARY ISSUES

1. Ethical Principles in Management

Definition:
Ethics in management refers to principles and standards that guide behavior in business decision-making and actions.
Managers must make decisions that are fair, transparent, and consistent with moral and legal standards.
👉 Important Line: Ethical management ensures trust, sustainability, and organizational reputation.

2. Roots of Unethical Behavior

  • Profit-maximization without accountability
  • Lack of clear rules and governance
  • Pressure from stakeholders
  • Personal gain over organizational interest
Understanding the roots helps in designing policies and controls to prevent unethical practices.

3. Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR)

  • Voluntary actions by businesses to benefit society
  • Friedman Doctrine: Focus on profit maximization within law
  • Arguments for CSR: Enhances reputation, stakeholder relations, sustainability
  • Technology-driven CSR: AI, Big Data, digital platforms for social initiatives
CSR Framework Example: ----------------------- Company Goals ↓ Stakeholder Engagement ↓ Social & Environmental Projects ↓ Feedback & Impact Measurement
CSR aligns business success with societal well-being, fostering long-term sustainability.

4. Technology & Contemporary Issues

  • Digital transformation for ethical and sustainable management
  • Entrepreneurship using ICT and innovation
  • Big Data and AI in decision-making and CSR
  • Emerging ethical dilemmas: data privacy, cybersecurity, AI bias
Technology expands opportunities but also introduces challenges requiring ethical guidelines and social responsibility.

📝 Exam Tips

  • Start with definitions of ethics and CSR
  • Explain roots of unethical behavior
  • Include diagrams for CSR framework
  • Mention technology-driven initiatives and contemporary issues
  • Highlight importance of ethics in management decision-making
