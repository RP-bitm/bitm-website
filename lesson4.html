The Friendship Bond

Author: Brown Parlee

About the Essay

“The Friendship Bond” explains the importance of friendship in modern society. The author conducted a questionnaire survey with more than 40,000 readers of Psychology Today magazine. The results show that trust, loyalty, honesty, and emotional support are the most important qualities in friendship. The study also shows that most people are satisfied with their friendships.

Important Questions and Answers

1. Why did social critics expect people to be dissatisfied with their friendships? In what way were they wrong?

Social critics believed that modern life makes people lonely and self-centered. They thought people would feel dissatisfied with their friendships. However, the survey results showed that most people are satisfied with their friendships. Therefore, the critics were wrong because friendship is still strong in modern society.

2. What are the two reasons given for ending a friendship? Which reason makes more sense to you?

The two main reasons for ending a friendship are betrayal (loss of trust) and having different views on important issues. Loss of trust makes more sense because trust is the foundation of friendship. When trust is broken, the relationship becomes weak and may end.

3. What rules of friendship do the respondents mention? Do you follow the same rules?

The respondents mention several rules of friendship. Friends should share their feelings, help each other in difficult situations, support each other emotionally, and maintain honesty and trust. Yes, I follow the same rules with my friends because these qualities make friendship strong.

Qualities of a Good Friend

  • Loyalty
  • Trust
  • Honesty
  • Supportiveness
  • Understanding

Word Meanings

  • Questionnaire – a set of questions used for research.
  • Respondents – people who answer a survey.
  • Betrayal – breaking someone’s trust.
  • Loyalty – being faithful and supportive to someone.
  • Confide – to share personal secrets or feelings.
  • Reciprocal – mutual exchange between two people.
  • Affection – a feeling of love and care.
  • Crisis – a difficult or dangerous situation.
  • Trust – belief that someone is honest and reliable.
  • Supportive – giving help and encouragement.

Conclusion

The essay concludes that friendship remains strong and meaningful. Trust, loyalty, honesty, and emotional support are the foundation of true friendship. Even though people sometimes feel lonely, friendship continues to be an important part of life.
