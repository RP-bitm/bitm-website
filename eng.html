Unit 1: The Trumpet of Conscience

About the Author

Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. was a famous American civil rights leader and Christian preacher. He worked tirelessly for the rights of poor people, African Americans, and other minorities. He believed strongly in equality, justice, and non‑violence.

Background of the Essay

The Trumpet of Conscience is based on Dr. King’s speech in which he strongly opposed the Vietnam War. At that time, the United States government was waging war against North Vietnam, while Dr. King was fighting against poverty, discrimination, and racial injustice at home.

Main Themes

  • Moral responsibility of individuals and nations
  • Opposition to the Vietnam War
  • Non‑violence as a way to achieve justice
  • Racial inequality and discrimination
  • Poverty and economic injustice
  • Social justice and human dignity

Summary

In The Trumpet of Conscience, Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. calls upon people to raise their voices against injustice. He believes that silence in the face of evil is itself a crime. According to him, true patriotism means working for peace, equality, and justice for all. He urges people to follow the path of non‑violence and take moral responsibility for creating a better world.

Exam‑Friendly Answer (Short Note)

Dr. Martin Luther King Jr., in The Trumpet of Conscience, expresses his opposition to the Vietnam War and highlights major issues such as racial inequality, poverty, and social injustice. He emphasizes moral responsibility and non‑violence, urging people to speak out against injustice and work for peace and equality.

What did Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. mean when he said that justice is indivisible?

Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. meant that justice does not belong to only one person, one group, or one nation. If injustice exists anywhere, it affects justice everywhere. According to his speech, he speaks against injustice done to the Vietnamese people, as well as problems like poverty, racial discrimination, and social inequality. He believed that true justice must be equal and fair for all human beings.
