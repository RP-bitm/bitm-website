📘 UNIT 2: PLANNING, DECISION-MAKING & PROBLEM-SOLVING (BOOK-BASED NOTES)

1. Planning

Definition:
Planning is the process of setting objectives and determining the best course of action to achieve organizational goals.
Planning is the primary function of management. It provides direction, reduces uncertainty, and prepares businesses to face future challenges.
👉 Important Line: Planning bridges the gap between where the organization is and where it wants to be.

2. Nature and Purpose of Planning

  • Forward-looking: Anticipates future opportunities and challenges
  • Goal-oriented: Focused on achieving specific objectives
  • Continuous process: Planning is ongoing
  • Decision-making basis: Provides framework for decisions
  • Reduces uncertainty: Prepares for risks and changes
Planning ensures effective use of resources, avoids waste, and facilitates coordination among departments.

3. Types of Plans

  • Strategic Plans: Long-term, overall objectives of the organization
  • Operational Plans: Short-term, detailed actions to implement strategy
  • Tactical Plans: Medium-term, department-level plans to support strategy
  • Contingency Plans: Alternative courses of action for unforeseen events
  • Standing Plans: Policies, procedures, rules for repeated activities
  • Single-use Plans: Programs or projects used only once
Planning Framework Example: ----------------------------- Organizational Goals ↓ Strategic Plans ↓ Tactical Plans ↓ Operational Plans

4. Planning Process

  • Set objectives
  • Analyze environment (internal & external)
  • Develop alternative courses of action
  • Evaluate alternatives
  • Select best plan
  • Implement the plan
  • Monitor and review results
A systematic planning process ensures informed decision-making and efficient use of resources.

5. Decision-Making

Definition:
Decision-making is the process of selecting the best alternative among available options to achieve desired objectives.
Decision-making is closely linked with planning. It involves analyzing information, predicting outcomes, and choosing actions that align with organizational goals.
👉 Important Line: Effective decision-making reduces risks and improves performance.

6. Decision-Making Models

  • Rational Model: Logical, step-by-step evaluation of alternatives
  • Bounded Rationality Model: Decisions within limitations of information, time, and resources
  • Intuitive Model: Based on experience, judgment, and gut feeling
  • Participative Model: Group decision-making with input from multiple stakeholders

7. Steps in Decision-Making

  • Identify the problem or opportunity
  • Gather relevant information
  • Identify alternatives
  • Evaluate alternatives
  • Select the best alternative
  • Implement the decision
  • Evaluate the outcome
Decision-Making Flow: --------------------- Problem Identification ↓ Information Gathering ↓ Alternative Evaluation ↓ Decision Selection ↓ Implementation ↓ Outcome Evaluation

8. Tools and Techniques

  • SWOT Analysis (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats)
  • Decision Trees
  • Cost-Benefit Analysis
  • Gantt Charts & Project Management Tools
  • Simulation & Modeling software
  • Technology-based problem-solving tools (AI, Big Data analytics)
Using these tools improves the accuracy, speed, and effectiveness of decisions in technology-driven businesses.

9. Problem-Solving

Problem-solving is a systematic approach to finding solutions for challenges and obstacles in business operations.
  • Identify the problem
  • Analyze causes
  • Generate alternative solutions
  • Evaluate and select the best solution
  • Implement and monitor results
Technology enhances problem-solving through data analysis, simulations, and scenario planning.
