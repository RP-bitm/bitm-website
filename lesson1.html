Heart Speaks to Heart Across a Cultural Divide

Author: Gail Saunders

About the Essay

This essay tells a real story about two teachers from different cultures who become close friends. At first, there is a cultural barrier between them, but later their friendship grows through kindness, understanding, and support.

Summary

The writer and another teacher work together in the same school. Their cultures have a long history of conflict, so they initially keep distance from each other. Later, the writer faces a problem with the school administration and feels lonely. At that difficult moment, the other teacher supports her. They begin talking, sharing food, and discussing their lives. Eventually, they become very close friends despite their cultural differences.

Theme

  • Friendship can overcome cultural differences.
  • Kindness and empathy build strong relationships.
  • Understanding others helps remove barriers.

Important Questions and Answers

1. Can friends be as important as relatives?

Yes. Friends provide emotional support, understanding, and companionship. Sometimes people can share their problems more freely with friends than relatives.

2. Can you be good friends with someone from another culture?

Yes. Friendship depends on kindness, respect, and understanding rather than culture. People from different cultures can learn many things from each other.

3. Have you ever needed a friend like the author?

Yes. When I was in class 10, many of my friends were from the Tharu community. At first it was difficult to adjust to their culture. However, one friend encouraged me to join their festival and helped me understand their traditions.
