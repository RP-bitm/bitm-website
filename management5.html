📘 UNIT 5: CONTROLLING (BOOK-BASED NOTES)

1. Concept of Controlling

Definition:
Controlling is the process of monitoring performance, comparing it with goals, and taking corrective actions to ensure objectives are achieved.
Controlling ensures that organizational activities are on track, resources are used efficiently, and desired outcomes are achieved.
👉 Important Line: Controlling bridges planning and achievement of goals.

2. Nature and Process of Control

  • Set performance standards
  • Measure actual performance
  • Compare performance with standards
  • Identify deviations
  • Take corrective actions
  • Feedback for improvement
Control Process Flow: --------------------- Set Standards ↓ Measure Performance ↓ Compare with Standards ↓ Identify Deviations ↓ Take Corrective Action ↓ Feedback & Improvement
A systematic control process prevents errors, reduces risks, and maintains organizational efficiency.

3. Types of Control

  • Feedforward Control: Preventive measures before activities start
  • Concurrent Control: Monitoring ongoing activities
  • Feedback Control: Evaluating results after completion
  • Financial Control: Budgets, cost control, audits
  • Quality Control: Standards for products and services

4. Performance Appraisal in Technology-Led Organizations

  • Setting measurable KPIs
  • Using performance management software
  • 360-degree feedback
  • Employee development plans
  • Technology enables real-time monitoring and reporting
Performance appraisal helps managers make informed decisions about promotions, rewards, training, and resource allocation.

5. Transparency and Accountability

  • Clear reporting mechanisms
  • Use of dashboards and ERP systems
  • Audit trails for decision-making
  • Ethical practices in monitoring and reporting
Technology enhances transparency and ensures accountability in decision-making, resource use, and performance tracking.

📝 Exam Tips

  • Define controlling at the start
  • Explain process with diagram
  • List types of control
  • Include technology-based examples
  • Mention transparency, accountability, and performance appraisal
