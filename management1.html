📘 Unit 1: Business and Management (Full Theory)

1. Business

Definition (Write in Exam):
Business is an economic activity that involves the production, purchase, and sale of goods and services with the aim of earning profit and satisfying customer needs.
Explanation:
Business is done to fulfill human wants and earn income. It includes activities like buying, selling, manufacturing, and providing services. Every business tries to create value for customers and generate profit.

2. Nature of Business

Business has several important characteristics that define its nature:
  • Economic Activity: Business is performed to earn money and wealth.
  • Production and Sale: It involves producing or purchasing goods/services and selling them.
  • Continuity: Business activities are done regularly, not one time.
  • Profit Motive: The main goal is to earn profit and grow.
  • Risk and Uncertainty: There is always a chance of loss.
  • Legal Activity: Business must follow rules and laws.
  • Customer Satisfaction: Success depends on fulfilling customer needs.
  • Social Responsibility: Business should help society and environment.
👉 In exam: Write 5–8 points with short explanation.

3. Management

Management is the process of planning, organizing, staffing, directing, and controlling resources to achieve organizational goals effectively and efficiently.
Management helps in coordinating human and physical resources. It ensures that all activities are done properly to achieve business objectives. It is both a science (based on principles) and an art (based on skills).

4. Characteristics of Management

  • Universal: Applicable everywhere (business, school, etc.)
  • Goal-Oriented: Focused on achieving objectives
  • Continuous Process: Ongoing activity
  • Group Activity: Done in teams
  • Dynamic: Changes with environment
  • Science and Art: Uses knowledge + skills
  • Professional: Requires training and ethics
  • Multidisciplinary: Uses different subjects
These features show that management is essential for proper functioning of any organization.

5. Functions of Management

Planning → Organizing → Staffing → Directing → Controlling
  • Planning: Setting goals and deciding actions
  • Organizing: Arranging resources
  • Staffing: Hiring employees
  • Directing: Guiding and motivating workers
  • Controlling: Checking performance
These functions are interrelated and help in achieving business goals efficiently.

6. Managerial Roles

Managers perform different roles in an organization. According to Mintzberg, roles are divided into three categories:
Interpersonal: Figurehead, Leader, Liaison Informational: Monitor, Disseminator, Spokesperson Decisional: Entrepreneur, Disturbance Handler, Resource Allocator, Negotiator

7. Evolution of Management Thought

  • Classical Theory: Focus on efficiency and structure
  • Behavioral Theory: Focus on human behavior
  • System Approach: Organization as a system
  • Contingency Approach: Depends on situation
These theories explain how management practices developed over time.

8. Technology in Business

  • E-commerce platforms
  • Automation systems
  • Artificial Intelligence
  • Digital marketing
Technology improves efficiency, reduces cost, and helps in better decision-making.

9. Business Environment

Business environment is the sum of all internal and external factors that affect business.
Internal: Employees, Management External: Customers, Suppliers, Government, Technology
Business environment is dynamic and complex. It influences business decisions and performance.

10. Porter’s Five Forces

New Entrants ↑ Suppliers ← Competition → Buyers Substitutes ↓
  • Competition in market
  • New companies entering
  • Alternative products
  • Buyer bargaining power
  • Supplier bargaining power
This model helps analyze industry competition and business strategy.

11. Business & Technology Interface

  • Improves productivity
  • Supports decision making
  • Enables global business
Technology and management work together to improve business performance.
