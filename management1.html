📘 UNIT 1: BUSINESS AND MANAGEMENT (BOOK-BASED NOTES)

1. Business

Definition:
Business refers to an economic activity involving the production and exchange of goods and services with the objective of earning profit.
According to standard business studies textbooks, business includes all activities related to trade, commerce, and industry. It plays a vital role in economic development by creating goods, providing services, and generating employment opportunities.
👉 Key Concept: Business is both an economic and social activity.

2. Nature of Business

  • Economic Activity: Business is undertaken to earn income.
  • Production or Procurement: Goods and services are produced or purchased.
  • Sale or Exchange: Goods/services are sold for value.
  • Continuity: Regular dealings are required.
  • Profit Motive: Primary objective is profit.
  • Risk and Uncertainty: Future outcomes are uncertain.
  • Legal Activity: Must comply with laws.
  • Customer Satisfaction: Essential for long-term success.
The nature of business highlights that business activities are systematic, continuous, and conducted with the aim of earning profit while satisfying human needs.

3. Management

Management is the process of planning, organizing, staffing, directing, and controlling organizational resources to achieve specific goals efficiently and effectively.
Management coordinates human and non-human resources to ensure the smooth functioning of the organization. It involves decision-making, leadership, and communication.
👉 Efficiency = Minimum cost 👉 Effectiveness = Achievement of goals

4. Characteristics of Management

  • Universal: Applicable to all organizations
  • Goal-Oriented: Focused on achieving objectives
  • Continuous Process: Ongoing function
  • Group Activity: Requires teamwork
  • Dynamic: Adapts to changes
  • Intangible Force: Cannot be seen but felt
  • Science and Art: Uses principles and skills
Management is essential for coordinating efforts and ensuring effective utilization of resources in achieving organizational goals.

5. Functions of Management

Planning → Organizing → Staffing → Directing → Controlling
  • Planning: Setting objectives and deciding actions
  • Organizing: Arranging resources and tasks
  • Staffing: Recruitment and selection
  • Directing: Guiding and motivating employees
  • Controlling: Monitoring and evaluating performance
These functions are interdependent and form a continuous cycle in management practice.

6. Managerial Roles

Interpersonal Roles: Figurehead, Leader, Liaison Informational Roles: Monitor, Disseminator, Spokesperson Decisional Roles: Entrepreneur, Disturbance Handler, Resource Allocator, Negotiator
According to Mintzberg, managers perform different roles to manage organizational activities effectively.

7. Evolution of Management Thought

  • Classical Theory: Focus on efficiency and structure (Taylor, Fayol)
  • Behavioral Theory: Focus on human relations (Mayo)
  • System Approach: Organization as a system of interrelated parts
  • Contingency Approach: Management depends on situation
Management thought has evolved from rigid, structure-based approaches to flexible and human-centered approaches.

8. Technology in Business

  • E-commerce
  • Automation
  • Information systems
  • Digital communication
Technology enhances productivity, improves communication, and supports decision-making in modern organizations.

9. Business Environment

Business environment refers to all external and internal factors that influence business operations.
Internal Environment: Employees, Management External Environment: Customers, Suppliers, Government, Technology
The business environment is dynamic, complex, and uncontrollable. Organizations must adapt to environmental changes for survival and growth.

10. Porter’s Five Forces Model

New Entrants ↑ Suppliers ← Rivalry → Buyers Substitutes ↓
  • Industry rivalry
  • Threat of new entrants
  • Threat of substitutes
  • Bargaining power of buyers
  • Bargaining power of suppliers
This model helps analyze the competitive structure of an industry and determine profitability.

11. Business & Technology Interface

  • Improves efficiency
  • Supports decision-making
  • Enables global operations
Integration of management and technology leads to improved organizational performance.
