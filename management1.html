📘 Unit 1: Introduction to Business and Management

1. What is Business?

Exam Definition:
Business is an economic activity involving the production, purchase, and sale of goods and services with the aim of earning profit and satisfying customer needs.

Explanation: Business exists to create value, earn money, and fulfill human wants.

2. Nature of Business

Write any 5–8 points in exam:
  • Economic Activity: Done to earn money
  • Production & Sale: Involves goods/services exchange
  • Continuity: Regular activity
  • Profit Motive: Main goal is profit
  • Risk: Chance of loss or gain
  • Legal: Must follow law
  • Customer Satisfaction: Focus on needs
  • Social Responsibility: Helps society

3. What is Management?

Exam Definition:
Management is the process of planning, organizing, staffing, directing, and controlling resources to achieve organizational goals effectively and efficiently.

4. Characteristics of Management

  • Universal
  • Goal-Oriented
  • Continuous Process
  • Group Activity
  • Dynamic
  • Science and Art
  • Professional
  • Multidisciplinary

5. Functions of Management

MANAGEMENT FUNCTIONS -------------------- Planning ↓ Organizing ↓ Staffing ↓ Directing ↓ Controlling
Exam Tip: Always write 5 functions with 1-line explanation.

6. Managerial Roles

MANAGERIAL ROLES ---------------- Interpersonal Roles - Figurehead - Leader - Liaison Informational Roles - Monitor - Disseminator - Spokesperson Decisional Roles - Entrepreneur - Disturbance Handler - Resource Allocator - Negotiator

7. Evolution of Management Thought

  • Classical Theory: Focus on efficiency
  • Behavioral Theory: Focus on people
  • System Approach: Organization as system
  • Contingency Approach: Depends on situation

8. Technology in Business

  • E-commerce
  • Automation
  • AI & Software
  • Digital Marketing

9. Business Environment

Definition:
Business environment is the sum of all internal and external factors affecting business.
BUSINESS ENVIRONMENT --------------------- Internal Environment - Employees - Management External Environment - Customers - Suppliers - Government - Technology

10. Porter’s Five Forces

PORTER MODEL ------------ Threat of New Entrants ↑ Supplier ← Competition → Buyer ↓ Threat of Substitutes
  • Competition
  • New Entrants
  • Substitutes
  • Buyer Power
  • Supplier Power

11. Business & Technology Interface

  • Improves decision-making
  • Increases efficiency
  • Supports global business

📝 How to Write in Exam

  • Start with definition (2–3 lines)
  • Write points with headings
  • Explain each point briefly
  • Use keywords: profit, planning, control
  • Draw diagrams if possible
  • Write 7–8 points for 10 marks
